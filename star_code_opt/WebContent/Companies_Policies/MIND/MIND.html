DO’S AND DON’T WHILE YOU ARE AT CLIENT SITE

 

 

Last Updated: Nov-07

 

Important:

 

Ø       Please carry all necessary Telephone numbers.

 

Ø       In case your flight is delayed or there is change in the schedule, please inform Client/MIND representative or any concerned person so that your pickup can be re-scheduled accordingly.

 

Ø       Please check your ‘Visa’ and ‘Period of stay’ validity dates.

 

  1. Be on time for meetings, discussions.

 

  1. Business cards should be presented and received with both hands. When presenting your card, make sure that the card is presented with the appropriate side up from the recipient's point of view. When receiving a business card, it should be handled with care. If you are sitting at a conference or other type of table, the card is usually placed on the table in front of you for the duration of the meeting. 

 

  1. Follow a system of hierarchy. Respect seniority.

 

  1. If somebody greets you like say ‘Good Morning’, you must respond to him/her. If somebody help you or do something special for you, say ‘Thank you’ very often.

 

  1. NEVER ask salary of your colleague (client).

 

  1. Friendly taps on the shoulder are never recommended.

 

  1. Eatables are not allowed during working hours. 

 

  1. Drinks can be bought anytime from the vending machines installed in the common area.

 

  1. While disposing trash, use respective bins. When in doubt, please ask.

 

  1. Avoid using Internet during working hours for personal usage. Browsing official sites is ok.

 

  1. Avoid shouting loudly to get someone’s attention--wave, or go up to him.

 

  1. Avoid wearing denim jeans in the office.

 

  1. Smoking is allowed only in the designated area(s) in the office.

 

  1. If carrying a Laptop, please bring suitable plug.

 

  1. Be prepared to tell your age. This might be the first question you are asked during an informal chat.

 

  1. Knowledge of a few commonly used terms in local language would be beneficial.

 

  1. Please carry basic medicines for stomach, fever, headache, cold etc.

 

  1. Please keep your apartment/hotel room clean.

 

  1. Be cautious while using electrical appliances. Do not put hot iron, cooking pan etc on the carpet or floor.

 

  1. Please take care of apartment equipment. In case of damage, inform immediately to your Team Leader.

 

  1. Preferably exchange TC/Currency at the airport.

 

  1. Countries prohibit carrying of poultry/milk products and fruits. Please check the rules before carrying these items into the overseas country.

 

Caution points especially for Japan:

 

  1. After exchange business card, introduce your name, period of your visit, purpose of your visit, say ‘Yoroshiku Onegai Shimasu (I’m glad to meet you)’.

 

  1. In case of Japan: People are called by their family name to which one adds the suffix “san”, but NEVER use this after your own name.

 

  1. Do not give tips in cafes, restaurants.

 

  1. Do not use your chopsticks to point at somebody.

 

  1. Do not leave your chopsticks standing up out of your food.

 

  1. Do not forget to carry umbrella to Japan.

 

  1. Since you will commute by bicycle from your Hotel/Apartment, it is advisable to carry hand gloves and cap during winter (Dec~Mar).

 

  1. Shoes are not allowed inside Japanese house/Apartment room.

 

  1. Garbage disposal is twice a week. Please check for the days at the time of check-in.

 

  1. For making personal calls to home etc., telephone cards are available at convenient stores. Minimum card value is JPY 2,000 and it gives you a call time of 60 minutes (approx) from Japan to India. Usage Instructions are available in English, Hindi and Bengali also.

 

  1. If coming for a longer duration (> 1month), part of currency can be exchanged at Yokkaichi also. However you must bring some Japanese currency (exchange at MATA) due to bank is closed in early morning and late night.

Note:

·         Indian Rupees cannot be exchanged at Nagoya Airport.

·         In Japan there is a 5% consumption tax. It is placed on every product you buy and every service (except public transportation).

Police - 110 (No area code required)

 

Dial 110 to call the police to report a crime, theft, disturbance or traffic accident. This is a nationwide number and can be called 24 hours a day. The call is free of charge.

 

Fire, Injury or sudden illness - 119 (No area code required)

 

Dial 119 to report a fire or to call an ambulance. This is a nationwide number and can be called 24 hours a day. The call and ambulance service are free of charge.
